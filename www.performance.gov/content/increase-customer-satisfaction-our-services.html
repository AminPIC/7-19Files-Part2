FY 16-17: Agency Priority Goal

Increase customer satisfaction with our services

Priority Goal

Increase customer satisfaction with our services

Increase customer satisfaction with our services by: 1. For the eight agency services included in the ForeSee eGovernment Satisfaction Index, ensuring that our customers continue to rate our internet services above the excellent threshold (over 80 points) on average, with a FY 2016 average of at least 84.5 and a FY 2017 average of at least 85. 2. Ensuring that our customers rate our office and telephone services as Excellent, Very Good, or Good (E/VG/G), with a FY 2016 target of 80%, and a FY 2017 target of 80%.

Goal Overview

Customer relationships with Social Security span a lifetime and are supported by access to accurate, real-time, and secure information and services.  Our vision is that enabled by technology and our employees, our customers have real-time access and customer engagement when and where they need us. Customer choice of where and how they receive service is important. Wage earners, retirees, survivors, and individuals with disabilities and their loved ones come to our agency seeking immediate assistance, care, and help. Other customers, including employers, businesses, non-profits, advocates, oversight groups and other stakeholders, also require our attention and support.

We have a long history of exemplary customer service with high customer satisfaction ratings.  In surveys and anecdotally through social media and other forums, many of our customers have praised the service we provide.  Our goal is to achieve even greater success in customer service and satisfaction through innovative online self-service options.

The strategies and milestones in this plan are designed to ensure our customers are highly satisfied, and that we solicit and incorporate feedback from our customers to improve customer satisfaction.

Strategies

We routinely evaluate how customers choose to conduct their business and the customers' satisfaction with our service.  We employ tools including real-time surveys of our Internet users, rigorous annual surveys, and recently implemented post-call surveys of our telephone customers.  Additionally, pending receipt of the equipment, we are participants in the Federal Feedback Button Pilot, with kiosks placed in Card Centers.  We evaluate the information collected to implement a range of improvements to meet our customers' diverse needs and preferences.

Customer satisfaction scores for our top internet services consistently range between 88-91, putting them at the top of the ForeSee eGovernment Satisfaction Index, and often exceeding the best private sector sites.  Scores above 80 are considered excellent, with highly satisfied customers.  Because customer expectations for internet services increase over time, maintaining a consistent score requires regular improvements in a site's information, navigation, and functionality.  Improving scores requires a more significant investment in making the right changes.   We will leverage the analysis from our surveys and target the changes expected to have the most significant impact on customer satisfaction.  Additionally, we will evaluate our internet services with the lowest two scores to determine and evaluate actions needed to increase customer satisfaction for those services.

In order to increase customer satisfaction, we will employ the following strategies:

Regularly review the results of our ForeSee internet customer satisfaction surveys (per the quarterly milestones identified below), identify improvements that should improve customer satisfaction and take appropriate actions.

Review analysis from prior ForeSee surveys to identify additional actions for implementation.

Review results from the Field and Hearing Office Visitor Survey, 800 Number Caller Survey, Field Office Caller Survey, and Social Security Card Center Visitor Survey and take appropriate actions.

Conduct Post-Call Surveys for customers of our National 800 Number, collect and analyze customer feedback, and take appropriate actions.

After receipt of the equipment, implement the Federal Feedback Button Pilot in Card Centers.

Progress Update

We have two primary indicators to measure customer satisfaction:

  •  Customer Satisfaction with Online Services
  • Customer Satisfaction with Office and Telephone Services.

 We provide the opportunity for our customers to respond to our online survey and the field and telephone survey so we may gain vital feedback about all the services we offer and make enhancements where necessary.  We review the responses from both surveys throughout the year, and we try to incorporate updates continually to improve customer satisfaction.

Online service satisfaction rating comes from ForeSee's e-Government report card.  The Customer Satisfaction rating is calculated by averaging the scores of our eight online services.  All eight online service scores are equally weighted in determining the overall average.

The Overall SSA Customer Satisfaction Score for fourth quarter FY 2016 was 84.1.  The FY 2016 FYTD score was 84.9, which exceeds the FY 2016 goal of 84.5.

For FY 2016 fiscal year to date, five of the eight online applications continue to score an 85 or above and seven applications exceeded the ForeSee Customer Satisfaction Excellence threshold with scores of 80 or higher.  While Business Services Online increased by one point, Extra Help with Medicare Prescription Costs, iClaim and my Social Security continue to maintain their Q3 scores.  Retirement Estimator, Disability and iAppeals dropped by one point.  The eight individual application scores are:

The iClaim and iClaim-Disability Satisfaction Inquiry Requests (SIRs) were conducted in the third quarter of FY 2016.  The iClaim analysis found that users are confused when applying for Medicare Parts A and B.  To assist our users, we are exploring additional ways of providing general Medicare information as well as links to Medicare.gov to ensure clarity and ease of the application process. We will continue to find ways to improve the overall customer satisfaction experience with each new release of the online applications.

Performance Indicators

ForeSee E-Government Satisfaction Index

Each quarter, ForeSee publishes the E-Government Satisfaction Index, which is a comprehensive reflection of the citizen experience with federal government websites.  It serves as a critical checkpoint for evaluating the success and performance of the federal government’s online initiatives and provides a consistent measure over time and across the federal government.

ForeSee measures customer satisfaction on a 0–100 scale, with 69 and below indicating the need for improvement, 70-79 as average (satisfied customers), and above 80 as excellent (highly satisfied customers).  The highest scores of any websites are typically no higher than 90-91.

SSA has eight internet services included in the ForeSee E-Government Satisfaction Index, with recent scores as follows:

  • SSA Main Website (75)
  • my Social Security (87)
  • iClaim (88)
  • iClaim Disability (82)
  • Retirement Estimator (90)
  • iAppeals (76)
  • Extra Help with Medicare Prescription Drug Plans (91)
  • Business Services Online  (85)

2010-Q4 September, 2010 83.5 78.2

The FY 2010 combined overall service satisfaction rate declined significantly to 78.2 percent from the previous rate of 81 percent E/VG/G, which held steady for several years through FY 2009.

The performance indicator is based on the combined result of the Office of Quality Performance's (OQP) annual service satisfaction surveys of 800 number callers, field office (FO) callers, office visitors (including both FO and hearing office visitors) and individuals who used transactional services online (in FY 2010, an “iApplication;” i.e., iClaim or i1020 for Medicare Part D subsidy). The performance indicator reflects the percent of individuals rating SSA service as excellent, very good, or good (E/VG/G) on SSA's standard six-point rating scale: Excellent, very good, good, fair, poor, very poor.
2011-Q4 September, 2011 83.5 81.4

The FY 2011 combined overall service satisfaction rate was 81.4 percent E/VG/G. This rate reversed the statistically significant decline that occurred in FY 2010, when overall satisfaction dropped to 78.2 percent. The FY 2011 rate represented a return to the satisfaction level that prevailed for the last 5 years.

The performance indicator is based on the combined result of the Office of Quality Performance's (OQP) annual service satisfaction surveys of 800 number callers, field office (FO) callers, office visitors and individuals who used transactional services online. The FY 2011 survey introduced several new customer segments into the performance indicator: Social Security Card Center (SSCC) visitors were included in the Office Visitor Survey along with FO and hearing office visitors. The Internet Report Card Survey added Medicare-only iClaims to the “iApplication” segment (along with all other types of iClaims and i1020) and introduced the iChange segment with change of address and direct deposit actions.

Contributing Programs & Other Factors

Important influencing factors for this goal are information security and ease of use.  Executive Order (EO) 13681, 'Improving the Security of Consumer Financial Transactions,' has established requirements that will increase security but may have an adverse impact on ease of use.  As of July 8, 2015, OMB had not published guidance for the EO, but implementation of a second factor, regardless of the guidance, will require additional steps in both the registration process and for each login.  While the additional security could have a positive impact on customer satisfaction, the additional complexity in the registration and login processes might have the opposite impact.
